Dimes

Overview

Dimes was created to ease the stress and intimidation that comes from finances and budgeting. Our easy-to-use platform and engaging design make the path to financial literacy enjoyable and gratifying.

Objective

Dimes is a native iOS web application designed to provide an enjoyable and upbeat approach to organizing finances, managing budgets, and growing savings.

Challenge

The biggest challenge in designing this finance app was presenting the necessary information while also evoking a professional, and trustworthy tone that is inviting to users beginning their financial management journey.

The color paletteâ€™s bright green signifies growth and wealth while retaining a youthful style, whereas the dark blue gives the app a calm, serious tone. The font, Quicksand, was chosen for its rounded forms to help soften the intimidation people may feel when it comes to managing their finances.

Visual Elements

The primary colors are used for the background, body text, and headlines to provide a professional and calm tone.

The secondary colors are used to invigorate the user and denote money. Visually, they are used as accents in the design.

These are the acceptable forms of the Dimes logo. The logo should not be stretched, distorted, filtered, or altered. This includes changing the colors, using different fonts, or only using the brand name text.

Process

The process began with researching apps that are already on the market.

With a clear view of the brand's tone and what competitors on the market were doing, I established the design elements and color palette of Dimes in a branding Guide.

I moved to creating preliminary sketches and establishing a user flow diagram to visualize and organize important features and key tasks for users.

User Flow

Low-Fi Wireframes

When the initial ideas were sketched and user flows were distinguished, I created a low-fidelity wireframe prototype for users to test. Since I decided that the Overview screen would have almost the necessary content in one interface, users completing the tasks felt the design was very straightforward.

Final Screens

The feedback I received centered around important features like showing an extensive view of the Budget section or including more information on each transaction, namely in which budget section categorized the purchase and the date.

I reviewed the critique and made the appropriate edits to the design and added the remaining design elements and color palette, resulting in a finished UI design for Dimes.
